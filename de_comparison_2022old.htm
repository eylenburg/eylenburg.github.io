Comparison of Linux and Unix Desktop Environments

The below table shows a comparison of the main 15 desktop environments available for Linux and Unix.

There are the Big 2 - KDE and GNOME - as well as some smaller ones and lots of forks and variants of the big ones.

Especially since the controversial initial release of GNOME 3, there have been lots of variants and forks: Unity, Mate, Cinnamon, as well as Solus's Budgie, and elementaryOS's Pantheon (the latter two are not forks strictly speaking, however they use some core parts of the Gnome stack, e.g. Mutter as window manager or Gnome apps). Unity has been dropped by Canonical and is now developed by the community, renamed to UnityX (Unity 7 for desktops) and Lorimi(Unity 8 for phones), which are the defaults in the Ubuntu Unity and Ubuntu Touch distributions, respectively.

KDE only had one notable fork, Trinity, which is based on KDE 3.

Other notable desktop environments include LXQt (a merger of LXDE and Razor-qt), Deepin Desktop, Lumina, and Enlightenment and its fork Moksha.
