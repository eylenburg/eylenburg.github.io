Monitoring Activity in Altchans

Background

On 16th April 2025, 4chan went offline following a hack from someone associated with Soyjak Party, another imageboard. The hacker leaked the source code and the emails of the janitors and soon after the 4chan admins took the server offline. When or if the site will go online again is not sure at the moment, but there are several "altchans" (alternatives to 4chan). Now, 1 week after 4chan went offline, I had a look around the various altchans to see where users have found refuge. Below, I have listed several altchans and their posts per day by board.

What's an imageboard?

It is a bit like a forum but with several important differences:

  • Users can (and are encouraged to) upload pictures or videos alongside their posts, even if the picture doesn't add value to the discussion.
  • User post anonymously, with no accounts or usernames. This means discussion are both more honest, with people voicing their real opinions on controversial topics (as you don't have a "reputation" to keep) and at the same time it encourages shitposting, trolling, pretending to be multiple people and so on.
  • There are no likes, there is no karma, and if you ever get banned you just come back with a new IP. This means people feel free to speak their mind and don't need to fear the jannies or the opinion of the majority.
  • Posts are ephemeral; there are a limited amount of allowed threads on any given board and for each new thread made an old one gets deleted or archived.

Inclusion criteria

  1. The majority of the posting needs to be in the English language. This excludes boards like the Japanese 2ch, the Polish Ziemniachan, or the Finnish Ylilauta (although the latter has an /int/ board).
  2. It must have relevant boards that are shown in the table below, so for example an imageboard that has only an /mlp/ equivalent wouldn't be shown because I have only compared stats for a subset of 4chan boards.
  3. For a board to show it needs to have at least 100 posts per day). Boards that only one strong board (e.g. /b/) will be shown in the "other" column.

Caveats

  • Posting activity is always just a snapshot and will fluctuate during different times of the day or might be higher on certain days e.g. weekends.
  • Some of the traffic might be from 4chan refugees, but some of the boards already had their own large communities before 4chan went offline.
  • Some boards have more bots inflating the post count, for example Soyjak.st has a relatively lax captcha.
  • Some boards on altchans may not correspond directly to a 4chan board, for example one 4chan board might be split into multiple smaller boards on altchans (e.g. /g/ becomes /tech/ and /prog/) or multiple 4chan boards can be combined in an altchan (e.g. /mu/ and /tv/ merged into a /mtv/ board). The former (splitting into smaller boards) might mean an altchan doesn't appear in the table below; the latter (merging into bigger boards) might overstate the actual use on a like-for-like comparison.

Data source

  1. marge.moe which tracks the posts of several altchans (thanks to the person who told me about this website!)
  2. An altchan's own by-board "post per hour/day" (PPH/PPD) overview, if available. PPH is less useful as it will fluctuate during different times, but often only PPH is published.
  3. If no statistics are available, the next option is looking at the number of the latest post at different days and times to calculate the posting frequency.
  4. As a last resort, which only applies to Ylilauta where post numbers appear to be global rather than by board, it would be possible to manually count the number of threads and replies made in the last x hours and calculate the PPD from there.

So, what's the conclusion?

The most active overall boards are Soyjak.st and 8chan.moe with a large margin. However, a large share of 8chan's traffic is for the video game boards and a large share of Soyjak Party's traffic is for the soyjak meme boards - or just bot spam. Following up is Kohlchan with it's large English-language /int/ board. Other

The hobby & interest boards are still quite slow on all of the altchans and there's no real replacement for 4chan yet. If you were active on boards like /g/, /fit/, /o/, /an/, /fa/, /sci/, /his/, /k/, /biz/, /lit/ and so on, there's not a great alternative to these places. The altchans are still quite slow for these interests and other popular places like Reddit have anti-features like default sorting of comments by upvotes, mandatory usernames, "karma", and insufferable powermods.
